# NYC-Traffic-Collision-Analysis
### Project Overview
Traffic accidents remain one of the leading causes of injuries and fatalities in major cities. This project analyzes over 238,000 reported traffic collisions in New York City between 2021 and 2022 to uncover accident trends, identify high-risk locations and periods, determine the leading contributing factors, and recommend data-driven strategies for improving road safety.
![](https://github.com/Blarkswan/NYC-Traffic-Collision-Analysis/blob/main/NYC%20Collision%20Cover.png)
The dashboard was developed in Power BI using interactive visualizations and DAX measures to transform raw collision data into actionable insights.

### Business Problem

Understanding when, where, and why traffic collisions occur is essential for improving public safety and allocating transportation resources effectively.

This analysis seeks to answer the following questions:

Are there seasonal patterns in traffic collisions?
Which days and times experience the highest accident frequency?
Which streets record the highest number of collisions?
What are the leading causes of collisions?
What strategies could reduce future accidents?

### Findings

1. Are there seasonal patterns in traffic collisions?

March recorded the highest percentage of total collisions (approximately 10.5%), while accident frequency declined steadily towards December.

#### Insight

Traffic collisions appear to be more frequent during late winter and early spring, potentially due to weather conditions, increased congestion, and reduced visibility.

2. When do accidents occur most frequently?

#### Finding
Friday recorded the highest number of collisions.
The most dangerous time of day was 4 PM.

#### Insight

The combination of afternoon rush-hour traffic, increased commuter volume, and end-of-week travel contributes to higher collision rates.

3. Which street recorded the highest number of collisions?

#### Finding

Belt Parkway recorded approximately 3,700 collisions, accounting for approximately 1.6% of all reported collisions.

#### Insight

Although this represents a relatively small share of total collisions, Belt Parkway stands out as the city's highest-risk roadway and should be prioritized for targeted safety interventions.

4. What is the leading contributing factor?

#### Finding

Driver-related factors accounted for the majority of reported collisions, with Unspecified recorded as the largest category in the dataset, followed by Unsafe Speed and other behavioral factors.

#### Insight

The high number of "Unspecified" records suggests an opportunity to improve collision reporting quality. Excluding unspecified cases, unsafe driving behaviors remain the dominant known causes of accidents.

### Key Insights
- More than 238,000 traffic collisions were reported during the analysis period.
- Friday is the highest-risk day for road users.
- 4 PM represents the peak collision hour.
- Belt Parkway experienced the highest number of reported collisions.
- Unsafe driving behaviors remain the primary known contributors to accidents.
- Traffic collisions show noticeable monthly variation, with March recording the highest proportion of accidents.

### Recommendations
1. Increase Rush-Hour Traffic Enforcement : Deploy additional traffic officers and speed monitoring systems during the afternoon peak period, particularly between 3 PM and 6 PM.

2. Improve High-Risk Corridors : Prioritize road safety improvements along Belt Parkway and other identified high-collision corridors through enhanced signage, lighting, lane markings, and intersection redesign where necessary.

3. Address Unsafe Driving Behaviors : Implement targeted public awareness campaigns and stricter enforcement against speeding, distracted driving, and failure to yield.

4. Improve Data Collection : Reduce the number of collisions categorized as "Unspecified" by improving reporting standards and officer training. Better data quality will support more accurate safety interventions.

5. Adopt Data-Driven Traffic Management : Use real-time traffic and collision data to identify emerging hotspots, optimize signal timing, and allocate enforcement resources where they are most needed.
